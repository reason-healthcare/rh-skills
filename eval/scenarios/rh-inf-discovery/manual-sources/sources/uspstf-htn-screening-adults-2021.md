# USPSTF Hypertension in Adults: Screening (2021)

**Source:** U.S. Preventive Services Task Force
**Year:** 2021
**Grade:** B (adults 18+)
**URL:** https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/hypertension-in-adults-screening

## Summary

The USPSTF recommends screening for hypertension in adults 18 years or
older. Screening should be obtained by measurement outside of a clinical
setting for diagnostic confirmation before starting treatment.

## Key Points

- Grade B recommendation for all adults ≥ 18
- Ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) is the reference standard
  for confirming diagnosis
- Office-based measurement ≥ 130/80 mmHg warrants confirmation
- Young adults (18–39) with no risk factors may be screened less frequently
  (every 3–5 years for those with normal BP)

## Relevance to Young Adults

While overall guidance applies to all adults ≥ 18, the task force notes
that prevalence rises with age; targeted outreach to young adults at risk
(obesity, family history, race/ethnicity disparities) is warranted.
